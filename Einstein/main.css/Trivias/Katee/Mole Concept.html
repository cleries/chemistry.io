What is a mole?

 

               In chemistry, a mole is a standard scientific unit for measuring significant amounts of very small

entities such as atoms, molecules, or other designated particles.

 

Who pioneered the studies on counting atoms and molecules?

 

               Amedeo Avogadro, an Italian lawyer and physicist, pioneered the studies on counting.

atoms and molecules. This is called the Avogadro’s number after he concluded that a

mole of any substance contains exactly 6.02 x 10²³ particles. The term particles refer to the

atoms, ions, or molecules.

 

To determine the number of particles in each substance/sample:

 
 

a. Consider 1 mole = 6.02 x 10²³ particles.

 

b. Convert the number of moles to its equivalent amount in the number of particles.

 

example: How many molecules are there in 5.0 moles of H2O?

 

 

5.0 moles of H2O    x     6.02 x 10²³ molecules H2O    =    3.01    x     10²⁴

                                             1 mole H2O
